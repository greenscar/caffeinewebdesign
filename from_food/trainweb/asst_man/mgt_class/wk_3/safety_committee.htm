Managing the Safety Committee

     
     

Shari's Safety Program consists basically of two core elements: the Safety Orientation and the Safety Committee. While the Safety Orientation is designed to get new employee off to a good start, the Safety Committee's function is to maintain a safe working environment throughout the employee's career with Shari's. Also by contrast, while the Safety Orientation is manager driven, the Safety committee is employee driven. In this reading assignment we will be discussing the formation, function and and follow-up required of the Safety Committee.

Let's face it, the Safety Committee is usually a task that is given to the new assistant. This doesn't mean that the committee isn't important, quite the opposite. Managing the safety committee requires a lot of effort, organization and some creativity to keep the committee interested and active. Some stores look upon the committee as a necessary evil, other look at the Safety Committee as an opportunity to involve it's employees in the crucial task of helping to provide a safe working environment for all employees and assisting in reducing the high costs of worker's comp.

Formation:

There are very specific rules (federal) for forming the Safety Committee. Remember, this is to be an employee-driven program. The committee must be made of 3 management-selected and 3 employee-selected members. The manager in charge of the committee is one of the manager selected members. The manager then selects two other employees to serve on the committee. Typically, we like to ask anyone who has had a reportable accident during the previous term to serve on the committee - however, if the employee declines, we can't make them serve. The other

three member must be nominated by the committee and voted on by the store employees. You will find voting ballots in your Safety Committee Binder. At the first meeting the committee will select a chairman who will basically run the meetings and a secretary who will record and post the meeting minutes. At term on the committee lasts 6 month and members of the committee may serve up to two consecutive terms.

Safety Committee Functions:

The safety committee is to meet once a period. The Safety Committee binder for each store is divided into tabs (13): one for each period. Under each tab you will find everything that the Safety Committee needs for that period. These items are:

Calendar - schedule a weekly inspection and post
Meeting Agenda - Use to conduct this month's meeting
Safety Committee Meeting Notes - Post on employee bulletin board
Emergency Numbers/First Aid Card Holders form - Fill out if anything has changed
Hazards Observed - Post on safety bulletin board
First Aid Kit Requirements - Use to inventory and stock first aid kits
DM Hazard Assessment Form - Used for the DM's inspection
Safety Committee Weekly Inspection Checklist - 4, one for each week
Safety Inspection Checklist Follow-up - Use to record action items after inspection

The Safety Committee will use these forms and documents throughout the period to complete their responsibilities.

Safety Inspection: The committee is responsible for conducting 4 inspections during the period, preferably one each week. The inspections should be assigned to different members of the committee. Each employee will need to be trained on how to complete the inspection. To assist with this there is a training outline located in the "Shari's Safety Program" tab of the Safety Committee Binder. Click here to print off your own training outline. After completing the inspection the employees write their recommendations on the Safety Inspection Checklist Follow-up form. These forms are reviewed at the next committee meeting.
Investigate Accidents: The manager in charge of the Safety Committee is responsible for investigating reportable accidents within 24 hours of their occurrence. The committee will review the report at the next meeting. Members of the Safety Committee are also the eyes and ears for management - they should bring any concerns or un-safe practices that they observe to the manager's attention immediately.
First Aid Supplies: Another responsibility of the Safety Committee is to inventory and purchase stock for the store first aid kits. All items are ordered from the corporate office. You must stock the required items and can stock the optional items if you wish. Click here to view a First Aid Kit Order Form
   

Safety Bulletin Board: The Safety Committee is also responsible for maintaining the Safety Bulletin board. The items that must be on the board are:

  • Current Month's Safety Poster
  • Safety Inspection Schedules
  • Upcoming Meeting Agenda
  • Safety Training Topic of the Month
  • Emergency Numbers
  • Hazards Observed or Safety Materials Required Form
  • OSHA Log
On-Going Safety Training: The Safety Committee is also responsible for on-going safety training at the store. The training materials for this training session are included in the monthly information packet sent from the corporate office. The presentation typically consists of an information poster and a short teaching outline. On-going training is a federal OSHA requirement and must be completed each month.

Motivating and Following Up on the Safety Committee:

One of the most difficult tasks when managing the Safety Committee is keeping it active and motivated. As the manager in charge, there is a lot that you can do.

Meeting Schedule: Look at the members of your committee and try to schedule meetings on a day when most of them work - this way fewer employees have to come in on their day off. Also, give plenty of advance notice about the meeting so employees can plan. At your first meeting, you may consider having a set time for the meeting. For example: the first Wednesday of each period. Remember, all time spent at Safety Committee meetings or on functions is time worked and therefore paid. Click here to view a safety committee meeting agend.

Be Prepared: Have the agenda in advance and any copies that you need. You also need to be on time and not destracted by any other duties in the restaurant. Your committee members will only take the Safety Committee as seriously as you do. Remember, meetings should be no longer than one hour.

Follow-up and be Involved: By far the greatest criticism of safety committees is that managers don't follow-up on their recommendations. This causes the committee members to feel that the managers only have the committee because they have to. This is a waste of everybody's time and makes committee members feel used. Follow up on committee suggestions - even if the answer is "no", let them know why. Another opportunity to become involved is each week when committee members are completing their inspections.

Make Meetings Fun: Give lottery tickets to each member who has completed a safety inspection, give a door prize, have the baker make cookies, serve pizza, anything to make the meeting fun and out of the ordinary. Ask the committee what they would like.

Provide Training: Don't expect to delegate committee activities without providing training. Use the training outline for conducting inspections and conduct an inspection with each new committee member. Review the first aid kit orders and also provide assistance on completing the monthly training and keeping the bulletin board up to date.

The Safety Committee is important to insure that we maintain a safe environment for our employees and guests. In the long run it can increase the store's profit and improve store moral by having people involved and identifying hazards early. Having a safety committee is a state and federal requirement and a manager can adopt one of two philosophies: one is to meet the minimum requirements with a little effort as possible. The second is to realize that the time and effort must be spent so they might as well make it count for something by being involved and building an active and effective committee.
