Conducting a PPQC Worksheet

   
   

The Product Portion and Quality checklist, commonly known as the PPQC, is a tool to help you monitor the portions and quality of food items. When conducting a PPQC there is no gray area, the food item is either to standard or it is not. Of all the developmental tools available, the PPQC is probably the most important as it deals not only with portions (waste and profit), product quality (guest satisfaction and profit), but also, food sanitation (our guest's health, company reputation and profit).

The PPQC comes in two forms, a short form located on the Back of House Log that is completed twice a day. Today, we will be completing a long form which takes an in depth look at every food item.

To complete this assignment you will need to print off this form and place it on a clip board. As you go around to inspect each area, read the introduction section, it will explain in detail what you will be looking for.

Conducting the PPQC

On the PPQC form there are six columns. Some of the columns give descriptions for you to read, the other columns you will be filling in. Let's review each column.

Product Specifications: This column lists the product and describes how it should be prepped. For example: fresh fruit - 1" diced. Here you will also notice pack and portion sizes. For example: deli sliced ham should be in 2 ounce portions with 8 portions per pack. This is important for consistency of product served and inventory counting.
(DD) Date Dot This column notes the use of a date dots. Circle the DD if a date dot is present. Place an X across the DD if no date dot is present. This is important for product rotation and quality.
Under 2 Days (<2) This column lists the shelf life of each product. Circle if the product is within the listed shelf life or place an X acrossed if it is past. This is important for product quality and the adjustment of pars.
Initialed/Labeled (L/I) This column notes if a label and/or preparer's initials are on the product. Again, circle if present, X if not. This is important so that we an follow up with a person who prepared a product incorrectly.

Unique Standards

 This column describes the quality of the product, it is what you should look for. For example, shredded lettuce should be opened one bag at a time and no rust should be present. It is important that all product meet our standards.
Comments/Praise Here you should list your comments about the product. For example: if the lemons weren't consistent in size, perhaps they are cutting them by hand rather than using the lemon wedger. You can also write good comments about product. The more you write down, the more information you have to work with.

Back Of The House PPQC

On the back of the house portion of the PPQC you will begin by checking items on the cook line. You are specifically looking at product that is in use. Check all items on the cold table, steam table, freezer drawers, cold drawers and bread racks. Now, go inspect the cook line and make comments as we will be discussing our finding in the Chat room.


Table Tent/Meal Period Menu Items

In this section review the current table tent and meal period menus. List any products that have been brought in for these promotions but are not on our regular menu. Then proceed to check all areas as you did for the previous section.



Bakery Products PPQC

When checking bakery products, check both the pies out front and in the walk-in. You should check for both quality and portions.


Front Of House PPQC

When conducting the front of the house PPQC begin at the server alley. Check all items stored on the reach in and cold table. Next, move to the dessert station and check all listed items. Lastly check the condiments in several of the bus stations.
 

Now, log into the Chat Room by 4:00pm for a discussion of your findings. After the chat room closes, fax this assignment to your instructor.
