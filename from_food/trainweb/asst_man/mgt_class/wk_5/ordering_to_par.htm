Ordering to Par

 

What is "food cost"?

  • Is it what we sell?
  • Is it what we purchase?
  • Is it what we use?

Actually, it is neither and all of these at the same time. Food cost is a ratio of food used to sales generated and calculated using a beginning and ending inventory. The following activity will illustrate the true food cost calculation. Here we will be looking at two different stores with the same sales volume. One store is trying to control food cost by ordering less. The second store orders to par - let's see how this affects food cost. Using your calculator and the numbers listed, fill in the blanks.

Hopefully, this exercise helps you understand the difference between food ordered and food actually used. Please read pages 1-6 of the Inventory and Ordering chapter of your POS manual and then log into the Chat Room by 3:45 for a discussion about ordering to par.
