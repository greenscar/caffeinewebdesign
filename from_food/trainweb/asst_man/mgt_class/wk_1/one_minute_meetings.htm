One-Minute Meetings

The well prepared manager has completed the 15 minute check and has a list of “to- do's”, knows what today's goals are, and has formulated a plan of action for the shift in their FOH & BOH logs.  

Now, you must plan for these things to be accomplished.   You can't do it without your staff! If they don't know your expectations they will just muddle through the shift.   If they don't know your goals they cannot help you attain them.   There is more power in synergy (combined action) than in one working alone. If you gave each of your servers a different focus for the day versus the whole team working on the same focus what would happen?   When the whole team is working together for a common goal they can exchange ideas, motivate each other and attain that goal easier.   If everyone is going a different direction you lose synergy and motivation.

By completing your 15-minute check and filling out the information on the FOH & BOH logs you have made the first steps in preparing, organizing and planning for your shift.   Now it's time to organize and enthuse your staff to be a high performing team, and in only one minute each shift!  

 

From the manager's log you have the task of organizing the freezer and storeroom for the Sysco delivery.

You may be busier tonight based on the day shift sales. Prepare employees.

 

You have already chosen a training focus based on your store's goals.   During the one-minute meeting you can role-play selling appetizers with the servers.   This will help them create a habit.

Give the cooks some pointers on putting the appetizer up before the meal. Fire the appetizer first and for quick dish up entrees, sell the appetizer first.

 

If Sara and Ron are working tonight make sure to praise them during the meeting for doing a good job yesterday (you should have told them last night, also).   Catch people doing things right, or close to right and reward that behavior with praise. You are setting expectations and encouraging them to do more of the same. By doing this publicly, you are also telling the rest of your crew that you notice and appreciate high performance.

 

From this box on the FOH logs you have written the soups, specials and Mr. Clean project.   Include this in your one-minute meeting with the staff.   They can't sell the special if they don't know what it is.

Also from your FOH logs are your servers' shifts, stations, targeted break times and sidework.   Discuss this plan with the servers so they know what to expect.

Communicate to the host the Mr. Clean project.

Discuss with the cooks what line they will be working today and from the BOH log what Mr. Clean project they will be working on.   Communicate to the DMO the Mr. Clean project.

 

From your 15-minute check you have more information to discuss with the crew at the one-minute meeting.

  • Paper machines & phone dusted
  • Sidewalks swept
  • Find or replace Mary's name tag
  • Fill pie case
  • Remove old bread for pick up
  • Walk-in floor and pallets cleaned

Delegate as many of these tasks to the crew as possible.   This way they learn what is important to you and also contribute to the success of the shift.

 

You can use a piece of paper or a 3x5 card to make your one-minute meeting agenda.   You will develop one for the Front of house employees and another for the Back of House employees. Check items off your agenda as you cover them.

 

FRONT OF HOUSE

We may be busier tonight than usual.   If so, targeted break times will change

 

Discuss station assignments for each server, sidework, Mr. Clean and targeted break times.

 

Suggest 3 appetizers at each table.   It will increase your sales and tips.   Role-play:   selling to a party of 4.   “Hi. My name is Sally and I'll be your server tonight.   May I suggest an order of Nachos, Mozzarella sticks or our Sampler platter? They are all plenty for 4 people.

 

The soups tonight are Clam Chowder and Minestrone

 

The dinner special is our Riblet Platter.   Describe to Servers and have a sample available if it is a new special

Host-Fill pie case and dust paper machine and phone.

 

BACK OF HOUSE

We may be busier tonight than usual.   Make sure we are well stocked, have plenty of soup for dinner and are ready with bakers and reds.   Have back up ready to fire more bakers, if needed.

 

Discuss line assignments and targeted break times.   If we are busier tonight the break times will change.

 

We need to organize the freezer and walk-in for the Sysco delivery tomorrow.   Delegate one area to each cook

 

DMO-Organize the storeroom for tomorrow's delivery and clean floor and pallets in the walk-in

 

Cooks-how can we insure that appetizers are coming up before the meal? Get their ideas and you will gain their commitment to insure it happens.

 

We have some old bread on the line, please pull for bread man to pick up tomorrow

 

Ron—We all noticed the great job you did on the line last night.   Keep up the good work. Thanks!

 

 

“The best minute I spend is the one I invest in people.”   This is a quote from the book “ The One Minute Manager ” by Ken Blanchard and Spencer Johnson.   Who takes care of your guests, serves their meals, cooks the food and washes the dirty dishes?   Your employees!   Your people! You are the manager, so you direct them, coach them, guide and assist them.

 

Using the one-minute meeting on a daily/shiftly basis allows you to disseminate information to your staff that they will need to be top performers.   It also puts forward your expectations.   When this is used consistently your employees will begin to expect these things from you and realize how much easier it makes their jobs.   You will be seen as a leader with clear-cut goals and expectations and will earn their respect and loyalty, and they will strive to be top performers.  

 

No one wants to follow a leader with no goals—they don't know where they are going!   And a leader that doesn't make their expectations clear will have a staff that does only what it takes to do the job, no more.   Employees need to know what is expected of them in order to fulfill expectations; and praise in order to exceed expectations.
If today your restaurant was busier than usual and you did not complete your 15-minute check or one-minute meeting what would happen?

 

A.
 No one would notice
B.
 Chaos would prevail
C.
 The crew would volunteer to complete the check for you

 

The answer depends of what caliber manager you are, or decide to be.

 
 

(C).   The exceptional manager's staff would volunteer to complete the check. They know how important it is to the manager and how much better it makes their shift run. They respect the manager and are loyal because of the praise they get for a job well done and the steps the manager takes to develop them.

 
 

(B).   The good manager's staff would certainly miss having the meeting because it makes their shift run smoother.   Without the meeting the staff doesn't know what to do and they are in a state of chaos.   This manager isn't as good at developing their people and motivating with praise—so the staff does not complete the 15-minute check.   The manager finds time at the end of the day to complete the checks, but why bother now?

 
 

(A).   The poor manager that rarely does one –would anyone miss it?   The staff doesn't care because the manager doesn't.   They are just there to bring home a paycheck.

 
   

Which manager would you rather be?

Commit to planning, organizing, directing, coordinating and controlling your shifts using tools such as the 15-minute check, FOH & BOH logs, manager logs and one-minute meetings and you will become the exceptional manager!   Catch your employees doing something right and praise them; you will earn their respect and loyalty
