Setting Up The Back Of the House

 

The front and back of the house logs are one of Shari's greatest “disaster prevention” tools available.   Completing these logs at the beginning of a shift can greatly reduce the chance of the unexpected happening and also help you to PLAN a smooth shift.   A manager who takes the time to plan the shift is a manager in charge, as compared to the manager who “just lets the shift happen” and is continually reacting to situations throughout the shift.   As you get more experience it is easy to be complacent and assume that you have the experience to handle situations that arise or that you can check all of the items without the assistance of a piece of paper.   Truly, that is a rare manager.   Without a solid plan, Murphy's Law (anything that can go wrong, will) is sure to happen. Besides, the fewer problems that you have during a shift, the more time you have to devote to customer service, employee training and building sales.   Now what store couldn't benefit from that!

This may seem like such a simple section of the Back-of-House log, but this is where a manager truly takes charge.   If left to decide for themselves, the cooks will do what is easiest for them, not what is necessarily best for business – this is how you find your self if the middle of a busy lunch with only one line open.   This also gives the employees notice of what is expected; they know what their cleaning and station responsibilities are ahead of time and will work toward that goal.   This simple step of planning will make your life easier and also earn the respect of employees because they can see that you are planning ahead and not making sudden decisions.

This section of the log is really designed to help you ensure that those unexpected problems don't arise.   Our goal is to make sure that we are at optimum readiness before the rush.   In the case above, we would want to make sure that the dishes are caught up before 5pm.   We have also found some product that is close to its shelf life.   We want to make an effort to use this produce before it must be thrown away – this will help keep food cost low and give us insight into adjusting pars.

 

 

The Temperature Log is a critical tool that helps in the following areas:

•  Can alert you to equipment problems.

•  Documented proof in the event of a foodborne illness.

•  Relationship with the health department – inspectors love to see this.

This is a great task to delegate to your cooks.   Before you delegate, make sure that you train properly and then follow-up

 

 

List soups for the day, if soups are to change to use up leftover; list that also.

List any specials that you are running, remember, this is your opportunity to use up product that is close to its shelf life.   This can have a very positive affect of food cost.

 

 

In the “Notes To Staff” section, write anything that you think that you need to pass along to the crew.    If you leave this blank, you are really missing an opportunity to communicate.   Did you know that the number one complaint that we receive from employees when they call the office is lack of communication?   Always write something, even if it is just a thought or focus for the day.   This way employees get in the habit of looking at it each day.

 

 

The Quick PPQC is to be completed on both day and swing shift.   Look for poor quality or old product and prepping errors.   You can discuss the items that you found at your shiftly meetings.   Use the PPQC as a training tool by delegating it to your cooks.

 

Encourage your cooks and servers to write all wasted items on the Waste Chart.   This will help you identify training issues, excessive pars that need to be changed or procedures that are not being followed.

  Make sure that you follow-up with employees after each shift – remember, “You get what you inspect”!  

 

  

As you find wasted items in the trash, make sure that you list them on the Trash Audit.   This way employees will see the mistakes that are being made and take more care.

  You might even calculate a dollar amount for wasted items – this seems to mean more to the employees.  

Also, when you don't find any wasted items or see a large improvement, it is nice to note that as well.

Completing the BOH Log and all checks should be completed in this order. If the restaurant should become busy and you haven't finished your BOH log at least you have some of the most important things taken care of.

Never think because it's busy it's allright not to complete the BOH log. It can make a difference in the way your day goes - "Those who fail to plan, plan to fail". Don't be that manager! Come back to tasks and delegate others to trained staff. For example: one of the cooks could fill in the specials and soup section and you could delegate the PPQC to an employee trained to complete PPQC's. When they help the store succeed they feel a part of it and you've just increased morale and ownership!
