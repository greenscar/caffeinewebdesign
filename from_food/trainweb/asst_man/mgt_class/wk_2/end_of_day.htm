End of Day Reports




When completing End of Day you will count the graveyard till, combine the 3 shifts and prepare the deposit.   The POS system auto sequences (automatically prints) reports when you choose the function “End Day” from the office terminal.    The reports that auto sequence are: Daily Sales Log, Hourly Guest Count Control, Check Detail for Discounts, Late Clock Outs, Employee Open Checks, Critical Pie Report, Critical Items Report, Credit Card Batch Detail, Average Beverage per Guest, and a Managers Authorization report.

These reports print automatically because of their importance in helping you run your restaurant in the most profitable manner. During this session we will review these reports so you can become familiar with what to look for and how to apply the information you get from them.   The effective manager uses all the tools at their disposal to make the job easier and increase profit and morale.

 

Reviewing End of Day Reports:

In this section we will look at the end of day reports and what to do with them

Hourly Labor Control:

 
This report shows:

  • Sales per hour (Net Sales), how many guests came in each hour (Guests)

  • How many employees from each job code were needed each hour based on chart hours allowed (Server Support Chart, Cook Chart, etc.)

  • How many employees for each job code were working (Server/support, cooks, etc.)

  • Misc. lists store security, modified duty and support services that are paid hourly, for example:   A handy man that clocks in.


Review this report daily to find patterns in when the guests are coming in and when you are overstaffed or understaffed. You may need to make changes in the schedule to more efficiently handle guests.   If you are overstaffed you are wasting labor-if you are understaffed you are not giving exceptional guest service and negatively impacting sales.

Look at these areas of the above “Hourly Labor Control” report:

  • 6:00am-7:00am – Look at the server/hosts and cooks. What do you think is happening? Could the graveyard server and cook still be on the clock? I would investigate to see if they are waiting until the end of their shifts to complete sidework.
  • 1:00pm-2:00pm – Look at the cooks. It shows that 2 cooks are needed – they only had 1.4 cooks working.   What do you think happened?   It looks like a cook was on break during that busy hour. Not a wise move.
 
  • Look at the servers.   It shows that 2 servers are needed but they have 4 still working. We should look to see if this is a pattern and adjust the schedules accordingly. If this is not a pattern the manager didn't react fast enough to the guest counts.
  • 3:00pm-4:00pm – Look at the servers. It shows that 1 server is needed but there are 4.6 servers working. This is way off the mark! We need to find out why so many servers are working at an hour that is typically slow and provide remedy for the situation.
  • 11:00am-1:00pm - Look at the PPE for both hours and compare it to the staffing.   11-12's PPE is 6.52, the staffing guidelines call for 4 servers and the store had 3.6.   12-1's PPE is 7.45, the staffing guidelines call for 4 servers and the store had 4.0.   Is there a direct correlation between PPE and how well the store is staffed?   Absolutely!   If the servers are understaffed they don't have the time to spend up selling to their guests.   If you can increase your PPE you can increase your sales! For example: If for one week your average PPE is 6.52 and guest count is 4400 your sales are $28,688.   If you can increase your PPE by just .10 you have – 6.62 X 4400 guests =$29,128 in sales. An increase of $440.00 for one week - $1760.00 for the period! And all you did was increase the PPE.

  Because Out of Product

 

  • This report lists all voids made that day.   Review daily and look for items that should not have been voided – could have been a food or service comp.
  • Look for same items voided for one server – could indicate a training issue, perhaps the server can't remember to bring milk with the meal, or keeps hitting the wrong button.
  • Too many voids for the same person is a “red flag” that something is happening that shouldn't be. Investigate and correct the problem through re-training or counseling.

What does the example above tell you?   It seems that we are out of Mozzarella Sticks.   It also would be a good idea to check ordering pars and usage; we might need to raise the par. Pete entered the voids with the incorrect reason. Tina entered the correct reason. Was a note left in the logbook about the outage?   Did the manager manage to borrow some from another store? Do you need to find a way to bring some in?   We don't want to “86” a product unless we can't borrow or find a way to bring it into the store.

 
Check Detail Discounts:

 

This report shows all of the comps and discounts made for the day.   Notice the top row has employee meals.   Check to see that the correct menu item was ordered and the correct employee meal discount has been applied.  

The middle check in the second row is an honored discount. Check to insure the proper discount was applied.   The rule for honored discounts is the highest price honored meal and a beverage . If no honored meal was ordered then the discount is not to exceed the highest priced meal on the honored menu plus a beverage.   As you can see in this example the discount was applied properly.   In the next ticket we see a food comp because a “Wrong Price” was charged. This looks fishy to me-I would want to know exactly why we comped .54 cents on this ticket.

 

 
Late Clock Out Report:
 
 

 
This report show which employees clocked out later than scheduled.   Compare this with schedules and research as to why this happened.   Are we not scheduling properly?   Did a bus pull in?   Are they having trouble getting out on time due to sidework or socializing? You may need to counsel or re-train.
 

 

Critical Items Report:

This report is designed to help you with prep pars.   The report compares how many of particular items you sold yesterday, a week from yesterday – What it projects you will sell today – how many were sold a week from today, and the week before that.   You and your cooks can review this report before doing prep to determine if the pars for today are correct.   This helps prepped products maintain freshness and quality and will prevent wasted products.

 

Whole Pie plus Pie Slices:

This report is designed to help you with pie pars.   The report compares how many of each pie you sold yesterday, a week from yesterday – What it projects you will sell today – how many were sold a week from today, and the week before that.   You and your baker can review this report before doing prep to determine if the pars for today are correct.   This helps pies maintain freshness and quality and will prevent wasted products.

 
Average Bev Dessert Per Guest:

 

This report lists each server that worked for the day and what their sales are, how many guests they had, how many total beverages they sold and the average per guest, how many desserts they sold and the average guest per dessert (the lower the better in the guest per dessert category).

Cindy had the most sales & guests, the highest in average beverage per guest (Nichole is the host), but only sold one dessert out of 118 guests. I wonder what shift she works?   Must be the 6am-2pm server that says they can't sell desserts.   With that mind set-they will never sell any desserts. They aren't even trying.   So what can we do about it?   Challenge them, create some friendly competition, run a contest and offer a small prize!

 
Mindy had 68 guests but only sold 55 beverages. She did sell 6 desserts to her 68 guests, more than anyone else.   Can she improve in both of these areas?   Absolutely!   Challenge her, create some friendly competition, run a contest and offer a small prize.   The contest section of the “Marketing Binder” will give you lots of great ideas for contests.
 

 
Getting the idea?   The reports that print at end of day allow you the opportunity to identify areas that need improvement, problems such as possible theft and re-training issues. Put together an action plan for improvement, discuss at your managers meetings and get everyone on the same page.   You can accomplish more as a team (synergy) than one working alone.
