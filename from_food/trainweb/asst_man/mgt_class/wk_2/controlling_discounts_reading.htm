Controlling Discounts

Company wide, discounts average 3% to 6% of a store's sales. A store that does 135,000 in sales with 3% total discounts would have $4050.00 in discounts. That is more than the sales for an average shift! What if the discount percentage was at the higher end? 135,000 in sales with a 5% discount = $6750.00. This is why it is so important to make sure you are doing what you can to control and monitor discounts. This is not to say that we do not want you applying a discount or refusing a coupon. It just means that you want to monitor and control discounts as well as how they are being used.

At Shari's we use three types of discounts: promotional, employee benefit and sales deduction.  

  1. Promotional discounts are discounts that you need to monitor but don't necessarily control such as honored cards, bakery cards and marketing coupons.
  2. Employee benefit discounts are employee meal discounts you both monitor and control. These discounts are provided as a benefit to the employee. The manager uses his or her discretion in applying employee discounts.
  3. Sales deduction discounts are Comps that are provided to insure that every food item is accounted for. They allow us to not charge the guest as an apology for a food or service error while still accounting for the food used. Comps are discounts that we both monitor and control.   The way we monitor comps is to personally handle each guest complaint where a comp in involved and ensure that we've done enough to retain the guest.   Not handling the situation in such a way that the guest will return is a waste of a comp.   If the guest is happy with the resolution and will return, that is a comp well spent.   The way we control comps is to follow-up with our employees to ensure that the situation, either food or service, doesn't happen again.   During week one of class you learned about working the floor as well as guest complaints. You learned to use the front of house & back of house log to set up your shift and communicate with your staff. All this information is provided to help prevent problems from occurring. Remember to use your 6 th sense and you will eliminate the need to comp due to an unhappy guest. Your goal when running your shift is to keep comps at a minimum. Unfortunately there will be times when something will go wrong. This portion of your reading assignment will help you understand the different discounts available and when to use which comp.
 

When using discounts we are trying to accomplish three things:  

  1. Accounting for the use of food not accounted for by sales
  2. Coding discounts into proper categories
  3. Analyzing discounts to correct problems  

In the following section we will be looking at each type of discount and discussing when they should be used and how to follow up on them.

VOIDS: Voids are only used to take items off a guest check when no food is used !   Mistakes that were not prepared would be voids. For example, if a server entered the wrong item into the computer but caught it before it was prepared, the server would have the manager void the wrong item and enter the correct item.    Another example would be when a guest tells you at the register they didn't receive an item on their ticket, such as a milk.   No food was used, so no discount is applied; the item is voided.   In the forgotten milk example, there is a service issue and the manager should speak to the server about being more careful in checking the meal when it is delivered.
COMPS: Comps are used to account for an entire meal or part of a meal prepared that we do not receive payment for. This includes mistakes by cooks, servers and suppliers. We need to compensate the guest for receiving an item that did not meet our standards or the guests.   In this next section we will be discussing the different types of comps and when they should be applied.   You might be asking yourself “A comp is a comp, why does the type matter, we're just taking money off the ticket?”   The reason that we code comps into different categories is to measure performance and identify problems.   For example, do we have a server who typically takes too long to get to their tables, do you have a cook who has trouble preparing omelettes?   Also realize that the information about comps goes to the individual employee reviews that print at the end of the period.

RE-COOK COMP:   A re-cook comp is only used when something is served to the guest but comes back to the kitchen because the guest is not satisfied.

•  If a guest's steak was overcooked we would offer to cook another steak for them.   We would re-cook comp the steak dinner and enter a new steak dinner into POS. We have now accounted for the steak that was not paid for and also for the steak that will be paid for. Do not use the re-cook comp for items that do not make it past the pass bar. For example, the server does not want to take the omelette out to the guest because it is burnt. This would become waste. The food never made it to the table, the guest never rejected it.

FOOD COMP: Use the food comp button when there was something wrong with the way the food came out of the kitchen and you do not want to charge the guest for a portion or all of the meal. For example:

•  Foreign object in the food

•  Food took too long

•  The food was not prepared correctly and the guest does not want anything else.

BAKERY COMP: Use whenever you comp a bakery product regardless of the reason.    We comp all bakery items to one specific comp so that it will calculate in the bakery food cost rather than general food cost.

•  Cinnamon Whirls for a bake sale,

•  Donations of whole pies or

•  A slice of pie for a guest's birthday or anniversary.

GOODWILL COMP: You don't want to charge the guest out of the goodness of your heart. A GOODWILL comp always says “Thank you!” If you are saying “I am sorry” it is not a goodwill comp, it is most likely a food or service comp.   For example:

•  You want to buy a meal for a loyal guest,

•  You want to give a guest a sundae to celebrate an event.

•  The guest thought they ordered something different

Notice that none of these items are bakery items. Goodwill comps should be marketing oriented.

SERVICE COMP: The issue is service oriented and you do not want to charge for a portion or all of the meal. For example:

•  The server was inattentive, rude or forgot something that was included with the meal (salad).  

•  A full meal was entered and served rather than an Honored (comp the difference in price).

LIQUOR COMP: Use the liquor comp when you need to remove alcohol from the guest check. You cannot comp liquor for promotional purposes.   For example:

•  If a guest was served a beer and complains that it tastes stale.

•  You question the age of someone drinking a glass of wine, you card them and learn that they are not 21, you would need to take the wine and comp it because you cannot charge them for a product it was unlawful to sell them.

WALK OUT: Use the walk out comp when a guest has left without paying their bill.

•  Research the guest check before automatically closing it out to a walk out.

•  Talk to the server.

•  Make sure the guests have left the building.

The comps we have discussed so far are tools to fix guest problems and concerns. We will now go over the different employee discounts. Employee discounts are one of the benefits we provide to our hourly as well as salaried employees.  

EMPLOYEE DISCOUNTS:

Employee discounts are a benefit for our employees. Abuse of the benefit can lead to losing it.   Employee discounts are applied when an employee takes a 30 minute meal period break. Policies vary from store to store. Check with your manager to find out what the policy is for employee meals.

50% AND 100% DISCOUNTS:

The 50% and 100% discounts are employee meal discounts to be used when the employee is scheduled to work.   Managers must authorize all employee meals and close to either 50% or 100% discount depending on your store and district policy.   It is important to monitor what the employees are eating.  

30% DISCOUNTS:

The 30% discount is provided to employees after 90 days of employment. Their paycheck will have a 30% discount card attached.   Managers must authorize 30% discounts.   The key points are ensuring that the employee's signature and employee number are recorded on the guest check and placed in the till.   Look for dollar amounts per transaction, the discount is meant for the employee and their immediate family not a huge group of friends. The employee requesting a 30% discount should present their current discount card.   The discount is valid seven days a week, but only between the hours of 6:00am and midnight.

100% MANAGER MEAL DISCOUNTS:

Only manager can process this discount.   It is meant for your employee meal while on duty.   Be sure to check your store and district policy for off work meals.    It is important to watch the dollar amount of this discount group.   We want you to eat a nice meal, however, common sense should always be used.   Also, remember that you are an example for your employees.

PROMOTIONAL DISCOUNTS:

GIFT CERTIFICATE REDEEMED:

Gift certificates are not considered discounts however, it is important to ensure that they are closed properly, and the certificate is placed in the till.   Employees are to give back change on gift certificates

CORPORATE CHARGE:

Documentation includes guest check, signed and corporate charge account number is present.   Ensure that the person using the discount is an employee with corporate charge privileges.   Ask to see their corporate charge card if you do not know who they are.    It is important to ensure that you have a signed receipt for all corporate charges.  

HOUSE CHARGE:

This discount is for outside companies who have made arrangements to “charge meals “ at Shari's.   Documentation includes the guest check. Type in the name of the company and have the guest sign the ticket. Place the ticket in the till.   The guest with a House Charge account may also include a tip in the charge amount.

VARIOUS PROMOTIONAL DISCOUNTS:

Honored cards and BMGC's are our most applied customer discounts. The Honored card is provided to our senior citizens. The company will many times run a promotion that involves the use of a coupon. An example would be a newspaper ad or an on going promotion such as our Honored Cards. Company promotion discounts are processed under the “DISC NLU” button.   

Some of these discounts are “Smart Discount” which means that the POS system analyzes the ticket and deducts the proper item.   At times a customer may misunderstand the parameter of the coupon and become upset when the “Smart Discount” doesn't do what they expect.   In this case, the manager may override the discount to make the guest happy.   Even though the guest has misread the coupon, it would be a shame to spend all of that advertising money to get them in, only to disappoint them or make them angry.   It is usually best to accept or adjust the coupon. UNPLANNED DISCOUNTS:

Unplanned discounts are key areas Managers need to keep a close eye on because they can indicate theft or sub-standard performance issues.   Some examples of unplanned discounts are: server giving away beverages, desserts not entered into POS, discount abuse, creative pricing using open food, etc. It is your responsibility to review the “Discount Detail” report daily.
