Vickie Irish - Vice President

I began working for Shari's as a server in Bend in 1982, never intending for Shari's to be my career. I became an assistant manager in Newberg and for 6 years I was a general manager of various stores in the Seattle area. In 1988 I was chosen to create the training department. In 1992 I was promoted to the position of Vice President.

 

What do you like most about your job? I love my job because of its diversity. My tasks vary from day to day, teaching management and CEO classes, Guest Appreciation Weekends, producing videos, store visits and my all time favorite - opening new stores.

What is your favorite training tip? WALK YOUR TALK! Be everything you expect and more. Remember that each trainee is different and strive to learn something new from each one. Most of all - have fun!

What are your favorite interests and hobbies and outside of work? I like to read, listen to music, crafts, baking, bowling and I love to golf.

Esther Klein - Regional Training Manager

I began working for Shari's in 1988 with the intent of working part time to have a little extra spending money. I liked the values, beliefs and people in the company so much I continued to look and ask for ways to learn and develop. Here I am 14 years later; thanks to some very special people.

What do you like most about your job? Being able to share my work experiences and make a difference in other's careers.

What is your favorite training tip? When training a new manager, play the good host/bad host game with them. The trainee and the GM come in the door pretending to be guests. First the staff ignores you: time how long it takes for the trainee to feel they are not welcome. Then return and have the good host seat you properly. Discuss the feeling of each experience with the trainee. It really makes an impact.

What are your favorite hobbies and interests outside of work? During the summer my husband and I enjoy swimming and bike riding. I also like to see different historic and geological lights.
