  1. Heat pot roast in au jus. Cut one slice of white bread diagonally and shingle on a 9" plate
  2. Top bread with heated pot roast. Place a heaping cook spoon of mashed potatoes on plate and cover pot roast and potatoes with brown gravy.
  3. Sprinkle pot roast with cheddar cheese and fried onions. Garnish with a large parsley sprig and sprinkle parsley flakes over potatoes.

Using a format similar to the above write down the ingredients and portions of all the items used in your special. Even though you may not be an artist, sketch out a diagram of the plate - this way your cooks will know the plate presentation of the special. Using your Sysco order guide, price out each ingredient and total. To figure what to charge, divide the plate cost by the desired food cost using the following guideline.

Since our special will be served for lunch we want a 23% food cost - so the formula would be:

$1.50 ÷ 23% = $6.52

Now, we wouldn't want to charge 6.52, so we would round this to a normal price like $6.49. If we are not comfortable with this price we can calculate the cost using other percentages in the 21-25% range.

This is the rule-of-thumb process for costing out a speical but, not the hard-and-fast rule. For example, we may be trying to use up a discontinued menu item product. In this case, we may be willing to live with a higher food cost to provide a lower price point as an incentive to purchase. Selling the product at a lower food cost would be more desireable that throwing it out. In either case, it is important to spec and cost out your special so that you are aware of what you are doing.
