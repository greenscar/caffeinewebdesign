Invoicing



Accounts Payable is the term used to refer to the bills that are owed by each store.   The invoices (bills) that come into the store are posted in the RAS computer for tracking at the store level. The computer transmits this information to the corporate office each day through the day close procedure. The corporate office then traks and pays these bills.
Managers know the importance of monitoring and controlling costs in the restaurant.   Each month every store has a budget they work with to help maintain expenditures.   If you don't know what you are spending you certainly can't control costs and make profit.   After posting invoices the RAS computer system shares this information with the Budget Control Report. You can see by now that it is crucial that invoices be posted correctly.
In your Point of Sale Binder in the Accounts Payable Tab you now need to read the Invoice Entry section (pages 1-5) and review the chart of accounts (last page in the Accounts Payable section).   As you are reading write down any questions you have. You will learn step by step how to post an invoice in the RAS computer system, how to make corrections, and useful tips on posting credit memos, and much more.
If your POS manual does not have this information email your instructor by clicking on your instructor's email address.
