End of Week Accounting




At Shari's our week begins on Wednesday at 5am and ends Thursday morning at 5am. Some businesses begin their week on Monday and end on Sunday. But no matter what day the week ends the purpose is to keep track of accounting on a weekly basis.   We can have more control of our costs this way.   Imagine waiting until the end of the period to find out that labor cost is “out to lunch”!   There's nothing you can do about it then!   Monitoring costs is done on a daily and weekly basis to provide opportunity to keep costs in line.

In this section you will learn how to end the accounting week, what reports to send to the corporate office, the importance of the POS reports that auto sequence, the RAS reports that you need to generate each Thursday, and what to do with them.

End of Week Accounting Procedures:

End of Week - Checklist

Step 1  

Check open tickets

  • Swipe your manager card and check for tickets that have been in the system a long time (red tickets) and may need to be closed as a walk-out. Also, check for employee meals that need to be closed.   POS will not allow you to end the day if you have tickets in the system more than 4 hours old.

Step 2

Run “Clock-In Status” Report

  • Review “Clock In Status” report-Check to make sure that the listed employees are working, if not, clock them out and adjust time.

Step 3

Run a “Tip Report”

  • Use to pay server tips. After you end the shift or day you cannot run this report again.

Step 4

Press “End of Day” and exchange tills

  • To open drawer, swipe your card and press “No Sale”, gather all items and take the till to the office.
  • This will prompt the Sales Log for graveyard to print.

Step 5

End the day in the office

  • In the “Autosequences and Reports” click on “End Day”. This will prompt the Daily Sales Log, Hourly Guest Count Control, Check Detail for Discounts, Late Clock Outs, Employee Open Checks, Critical Pie Report, Critical Items Report, Credit Card Batch Detail, Average Beverage per Guest, and a Managers Authorization report to print.

Step 6

End the week in the office

  • In “Autosequences and Reports” click on “End Week”. This will prompt the POS Sales Log for the week, Suggestive Selling Report, Server Graphs, POS Flash, Workstation Closed By, Employee Open Food and Misc. Add Report, Time Card Adjustments, and contest reports (when applicable).

Step 7

PERIOD END ONLY

  • In “Autosequences and Reports” click on “End Period”. This will prompt the period POS Sales Log, POS Flash for the period, and Theoretical Food Cost reports to print.

Step 8

Balance Till

  • Balance to the POS sales log for the day. Additionally, verify and attach discount documentation to the Discount report. View the Managers authorization report, looking for anything unusual.

Step 9

Input numbers into RAS

  • Input numbers into RAS as for other shifts.

Step 10

Settle credit cards

  • Through the start menu, go to: Credit Card Settle” and click on the “settle now” button.

Step 11

Confirm Labor has been transferred from POS to RAS

  • Log into RAS and go to “Fix POS Labor Punches” under Payroll reports. Print the report and fix all errors listed on the report. They will need to be fixed in POS.

Step 12

Correct other Payroll errors

  • In RAS go to “Payroll Exception Report” under Payroll Reports. Print the report and fix all errors listed on the report.   They will need to be fixed in POS.

Step 13

Time Clock Shiftly Review

  • Run a Time Clock Shiftly Review from RAS. Confirm that everyone who worked the day prior had punched in and out.

Step 14

Day close RAS

  • Complete day close in RAS.

Step 15

Run RAS Reports

  • Weekly Sales Log (2 copies print – 1 for corp. 1 for the sales binder)
  • House Charge Summary
  • Cash Paid Out Report
  • Accounts Payable Recap (2 copies – 1 for corp. 1 for the weekly recap binder)
  • RAS Flash Report for your store
  • Budget Control Report
  • Labor Worksheet Summary

End of Week Reports

The reports that are Auto sequenced in the “End of Week” reports are a great value to the wise manager.   They provide opportunity to control costs by identifying areas that need improvement and track sales and labor on a weekly basis.

POS Sales Log – Weekly: This report is exactly like the POS Sales Log that prints out at the end of the day. However, this report totals the numbers for the week. One copy of this report should be sent in the Cash Management Packet, a second copy should be kept at the store in the Sales Binder.

Suggestive Selling Report: This report gives you information about individual server performance. It lists a server's net sales, number of guests, number of checks, PPE, check average, beverage sales, dessert sales, add ons, and appetizers and sides for a week. Review this report to see if any server is not performing up to same level as servers on a similar shift. Post this report on the bulletin board so servers can work toward their personal best in suggestive selling.

You can use this information for a daily focus when putting together your agenda for a one-minute meeting with your staff.

POS Flash: The POS Flash report gives you sales and discount information for the week. The report list percent of food, beverage, dessert and appetizer sales, dollar amount and percent of all discounts, PPE, turn times, employee hours and sales per man-hour. The report compares your totals against company benchmarks (the average of stores in the company). Include this report in your weekly discount packet.

You can use this report to set store goals to focus on areas that need improvement. For example: You notice that your store is below the company benchmark for ice cream desserts. At your managers meeting you can get an action plan together to improve this area.

Workstation Closed By:   This report lists guest checks that were closed to cash in stations other than the cash register. Use this report to identify servers who are not going to the till to cash out tickets and re-train or counsel them.

The only place we have a cash drawer is at the register; where are they putting the money?

Employee Open Food and Misc. Add Report: This report lists the amount of food that each server rang up using the “Open Food” and the “Misc. Add” buttons. Use this report to identify servers who may be charging incorrect prices for off menu items. Run a detail report to see the exact items. You could find that a server is using this function because she can't locate the proper button in the POS system, re-train them. You could also find that a server is giving away food to friends at a lesser price, counsel them.

Server Graphs: This reports graphs server performance in beverage sales, table turn times. PPE, dessert sales and add on sales. Post these reports on the bulletin board for your servers. Post stickers or draw happy faces on the high performers to get some competition going.

Time Clock Adjustments: Any time clock adjustments made during the week will print each Thursday. One report will print for each employee that has had adjustments made.   Have each employee review the report for accuracy and sign the report.   Keep in employees personnel file.

The following reports are generated in RAS and should be

run after completing Day Close in RAS (when running these reports you will need to change the date to reflect the week that is ending):

RAS Weekly Sales Log- (RAS Main Menu-Sales-Reports-Weekly Sales Log): This report is just like the Daily Dales Log except it adds all seven days together. It automatically prints two copies of this report: place one in the Sales binder for the store's records and place the other report in the Cash Management packet.

RAS Cash Paid Out Report- (RAS Main Menu-Accounts Payable-Reports-Weekly Cash Paid Out Recap): This report is a summary of all the cash paid outs you entered in RAS for the week. Notice that is a summary by account, not by vendor. Run a calculator tape totaling the seven days worth of Cash Paid Out slips. This total should match the total on the Cash Paid Out Report.

Attach the Cash Paid Out slips with the receipts to this report and include in the Cash Management Packet.

RAS House Charges Report- (RAS Main Menu-Sales-Reports-House Charges Summary): This report recaps all the house charges that you have put into RAS for the week. Run a calculator tape totaling the seven days worth of House Charges; this total should match the total on the House Charges Report. Attach all house charges complete with signed guest checks and any other paperwork to the report and include in the Cash Management Packet.

RAS Weekly Accounts Payable Recap- (RAS Main Menu-Accounts Payable-Reports-Weekly Accounts Payable Recap-2 copies): This report will group all the vendors together so you can see that all invoices have been entered for the week. It also breaks out the corporate charges, journal entries, credits and purchases. It gives you a total for each and then a store total. Attach the invoices in the order they print on the report to the recap and place in the Accounts Payable Packet.

RAS Weekly Flash Report (RAS Main Menu-Budget Control-Weekly Flash Report): This report summarizes sales, major categories of expenses from the Budget control Report, Labor house used vs. Labor Chart Hours allotted, vacation and overtime hours.

This report is primarily used by the District Managers for a quick glance of the performance of your restaurant. It's a good idea to run this report weekly to compare this periods' performance to last period. And you will know what your DM is talking about when they compare this period to last.

Budget Control Report- (RAS Main Menu-Budget Control-Reports-Budget Control Report): This report summarizes sales, expenses for each general ledger account, and controllable profit by the week. The manager should run this report weekly to keep track of how much is being spent in each account.

Labor Worksheet Summary-(RAS Main Menu-Payroll-Reports-Labor Worksheet-choose summary & give week start and end dates):   This will give you your total hours for each job code for the week.   If your District Manager requires an Hours Variance Chart, you will use this report to fill out this form.
