Scheduling Labor in POS

   
   

The POS scheduling feature in POS is designed to help your store manage labor. By inputting an employee schedule into POS, the system:

  • Prints the employee schedules
  • Keeps the employee from clocking in early or when not scheduled
  • Adds and totals weekly hours by employees and job code
  • Tracks the status of the employee's liquor service permit
  • Displays employee minor status on employee schedules

If the scheduling system saved only 5 minutes per employee by not letting them clock in early, or early from break, the cost savings is well worth the effort to input the schedule. For example:

12 employees (represents one day in a store) X 5 minutes = 1 hour

1 hour X $8.50 (average hourly wage) = $8.50 per day

$8.50 X 98 (number of Shari's units) X 365 (days in a year) = $304,045!

The original input time of an employee schedule is substantial, fortunately, this only occurred once. The POS system allows us to copy the current schedule over into the new week and simply make edits. The key to POS schedule input is to learn about the different edits and how to complete them quickly.

The purpose of this reading assignment is to become familiar with the POS scheduling feature. In your POS manual read pages 3.11-3.17. Also read the two pages of helpful hints that follow this section.
