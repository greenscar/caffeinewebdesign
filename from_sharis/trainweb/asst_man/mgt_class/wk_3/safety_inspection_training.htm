Shari's Management Corporation

Safety Committee 2003

Training New Members

  Preventing Accidents Through a Thorough Inspection

 

Material:          Safety Committee Weekly Inspection Checklist (1 per person)

                        Clipboards (1 per person)

Purpose:   To teach the new safety committee how and why to conduct a thorough weekly inspection. Use this as part of the first meeting after new members are elected or have a one-on-one training for any new members of the safety committee.

Who is to be trained:        The New Safety Committee                                          

•  Introduction

Each year Shari's spends thousand of dollars on accidents that could have been prevented with a little care.   It is the job of the safety committee to maintain a safe environment and the Weekly Inspections Checklist is a tool designed to point out potential danger areas.   Today we will conduct an inspection together, pointing out just what we should be looking for.  

•  Discussion

Prior to beginning the tour, make sure that everyone has his or her own copy of a weekly inspection checklist.   They should take notes on specific items to look for, while one person makes the notes for the official inspection for that week.  

Start on the cook's line and move to the prep area paying close attention to the following items:

Floors :

•  Is there dirt, debris or grease on the floor which could cause a person to slip and fall?   

•  Are any tiles loose or cracked so a person could trip over them?

Drains :  

•  Are the drains free of debris and odor which could cause a backup of water?

•    Are there covers over the drain so no one gets their foot caught in it?

Equipment:  

•  Ask the cooks if all the equipment is working properly.   Pay particular attention to the toasters and waffle irons.  

•  Are the steamers being cleaned shiftly?

•  Are the handles and gaskets on the reach-ins in good repair so that no one has to strain to open them?

•  Check the cords of all equipment to make sure they are not frayed.

•  Is there a minor sticker on the slicer?   Is anyone under 18 operating the slicer?

•  Are the cooks using and cleaning the slicer correctly?   The slicer should be unplugged and cleaned in place, it is too dangerous to carry it to the dish machine and rinse it off.

•  Is a slicer guard available and are the cooks using it properly?

Prepping:  

•  Are there at least two mesh gloves available? Are the employees using them? Do the gloves have all their fingers and have they been cleaned and sanitized?

•  Have the knives been sharpened within the last week?   People are less likely to cut themselves with a sharp knife.

•  There should be only one food item in the prep area at a time.

•  Is the cutting board being cleaned and sanitized between food items?

•  Is there a bucket of sanitation solution on each cook's line and in the prep area?   When was the last time the water was changed?

Now move into the Walk-in , Dry Storage and the Freezer

Floors:

•  Is there dirt, debris or grease on the floor which could cause a person to slip and fall?

•  Are any tiles loose or cracked so a person could trip over them?

•  Is there a build up of ice on the floor in the freezer?   This could cause a person to slip and fall, it also breaks up the tiles over time and causes them to crack and the freezer to be less effective.  

•  Check for visible signs of pests in dry storage, tracks in the dust, black droppings behind the pallets, or gnaw markings on the boxes.

Doors:

•  The doors in the walk-in and freezer should have an emergency release handle that glows in the dark and easily unlocks the door.

Storage:

•  Precooked items or items that won't be cooked should be stored above or away from raw items.

•  Heavier items should be stored on the bottom shelves so no one strains their back getting them down.

•  All items should be stored 6 inches off the floor and away from the wall.

•  Chemicals should not be stored in any of these areas.

•  CO2 canisters should be chained to the wall.   If a helium tank is present, it too should be chained to the wall.

•  All containers should be clearly marked as to what is inside so no one grabs the wrong thing.   Date dots should be present and initialed.  

Break Area:

•  The safety board should be clearly marked and have the required posters.   A list of the required posters is on the back of the Weekly Inspection Checklist.

•  The table and booths in the break area should be clean and securely attached to the wall.  

•  No more than one additional chair should be in the area.   More chairs will clog the area and cause a hazard if employees need to evacuate the area in a hurry.   Also, someone coming out of dry storage could trip and fall over the chair.

•  The first-aid kit should contain 2 Hep-Aid kits, a CPR shield, latex gloves and all the items listed on the first-aid kit inventory sheet in the Safety Committee binder.   Complete one of those sheets at this time.

•  Bio-safe should be available for employees to use twice daily.   Are the employees using it?

•  Are the employees using the hand-washing log?

Now move to the dish area , baker's station and backdoor area

Floors:

•  Is there dirt, debris or grease on the floor which could cause a person to slip and fall?

•  Are any tiles loose or cracked so a person could trip over them?

•  If your store uses mats, are they clean?   They should be free of any tears, nothing should be sticking up on them so someone could trip and fall over them.  

•  If water is present on the floor is it marked with a wet floor sign?

•  Is a broken glass bucket available and clearly marked?

•  Is there ice or water on the floor in front of the ice machine?

Equipment:

•  Have the filters for the dish machine been cleaned lately?   This could cause the dish machine to backup and overflow with water.

•  Are the arms to the doors and washer arm in good repair?  

•  The sprayer hose should be free of leaks and the nozzle should be tightly attached.   If this were to break it could cause water to spray all over the kitchen.

•  Does the DMO turn off the water running to the Expert between washes?   This could cause the water to over flow as well as use extra chemicals.

•  Rubber gloves and goggles should be available for the DMO's to change the chemicals.

•  Is a minor sticker present on the mixer?   Are minors using the mixer?

•  Is the mixer and cord in good repair?   Does the mixer attachment have all of its prongs and are they tightly attached?   These could fly off and hurt anyone standing near the mixer.

•  Has the oven been cleaned within the last week?   This could cause the equipment to breakdown quicker or take longer to bake food.

•  Are two oven mitts available?   Are they in good repair?

Storage:

•  The dishes should be stacked on the DMO shelves so that they don't fall off and hurt anyone.   Make sure the shelves are not wobbly.

•  The ice scoop should be stored on the outside of the ice machine.  

•  Mop buckets should be empty and stored in the mop closet.   Mops should be hanging upside down on a hook so they can dry.  

•  There should be several wet floor signs stored in the mop closet.   They should be in good repair, the letters bright and visible.

•  Are chemicals properly labeled?  

•  Is the Safe-Stride sprayer clearly labeled?

•  The hall leading to the backdoor should be free of debris.    

•  Heavy items should not be stored on the top of the ice machine.   This could strain someone's back getting it down.

Now move out into the dinning area

Floor:  

•  Is the tile area clean and free of water, debris, or grease.   This could cause guests as well as employees to slip and fall.

•  Are any tiles loose or cracked, so a person could trip over them?

•  Is the carpet clean and free of debris?

•  Are the edges of the carpet securely attached to the floor?   Guests could trip over the edges and fall.

Equipment:

•  Ask the servers if all the equipment is working properly.   Pay particular attention to the milkshake machine, syrup urns, and coffee machines.

•  Are the handles and gaskets on the reach-ins in good repair so that no one has to strain to open them?

•  The vacuum should be in good repair.   The garbage can that contains the vacuum should be clean inside and out.  

Emergency Exit Doors:

•  Aisle ways to the doors should easily assessable.   Chairs and High Chairs should not be stored in front of these doorways.  

•  The Exit signs should light up even in the day.   If they do not, a bulb may need to be changed.

Emergency Equipment:

•  Have the fire extinguishers been charged within the last year?

•  Are there 911 stickers present on the phone at the hostess stand?

Storage:

•  All closets out front should be clean and well organized.  

•  Items should be clearly labeled and chemicals should not be stored anywhere food is present.

•  Highchairs should be clean and neatly stacked.   Inspect the highchairs for broken straps.   If new straps are needed order them from SYSCO.   Broken straps could allow a child to fall out of a highchair.

•  At least 4 wet floor signs should be present. They should be in good repair and letters bright and visible.

•  Are the bleach bottles clearly marked?

  Now go outside

Dumpster area:

•  Are the containers closed?   This will cut down on pests and odors.  

•  Is the ground clean and free of debris and grease?   This too will attract pest and could cause an employee or vendor to fall.  

Parking Area:

•  Are the sidewalks in good repair?   Guests could trip and fall if there are cracks and uneven areas.

•  Are the curbs in good repair?  

Now return to the table where you started the meeting.   The committee members should be able to answer the following questions based on the employees they work with.  

Employees:

•  Do all the employees have the proper footwear?   If they do not have rubber soled shoes, they can slip and fall anytime they are walking on the tile, even if it isn't wet.

•  Do all the employees consistent say “behind you” when passing other employees?

•  Has any employee expressed a safety concern to any member of the committee?

•  Do the DMO's consistently wear back braces?  

•  Have you ever observed an employee doing something unsafe to themselves or others?

This tour should take approximately 20 minutes and should be conducted on a weekly basis.   Anytime an inspector finds anything that is unsafe this should be noted on the Weekly Inspection Checklist.

•  Following-Up

Once an inspection has been completed, transfer all the findings to a Follow-up report, which can be found in the Safety Committee binder.   This report should be given to the general manager so that corrections can be made.   Make a note next to each item that is a training issue so that this can be shared with the CEOs.  

•  Assignment

Decide who will conduct the next three inspections.   Write their names on the safety committee calendar that will be posted on the safety bulletin board.
